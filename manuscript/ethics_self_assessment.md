# Ethics Self-Assessment: Research Ethics Determination

**Study:**
"Evaluator-dependent conclusions in audience-calibrated medical LLM safety assessment: a paired multi-model benchmark study"

**Author:** Koyar Afrasyab, MD, Kinvectum AB, Sweden
**Date of assessment:** 18 July 2026

## Determination

The author has assessed both studies against the Swedish Ethical Review Act (lag 2003:460 om etikprövning av forskning som avser människor) and concluded that neither study falls within its scope, and that ethical review by Etikprövningsmyndigheten is therefore not required.

## Basis for the determination

**1. No processing of personal data (personuppgifter).** Both studies used exclusively (a) publicly released benchmark questions (Real-POCQi public questions split; HealthBench; MedRBench) that are deidentified by their publishers and contain no direct or indirect identifiers, and (b) text outputs generated by commercial large language models in response to those questions. No data were collected from or about any identifiable living person. Under 3 § of the Act, ethical review is required for research involving sensitive personal data; no such data were processed.

**2. No physical or psychological intervention on human subjects.** No patients, volunteers, or other human participants were recruited, contacted, observed, or exposed to any procedure (4 § of the Act). The studies are computational benchmarks of software systems.

**3. No biological material from humans.** Not applicable (4 § of the Act).

**4. Source-data provenance.** The Real-POCQi questions originate from queries submitted by physicians in the course of clinical work and were released publicly by the dataset authors in deidentified form under their own governance. This study performed secondary computational analysis of the already-public split only, made no attempt at reidentification, and redistributes no source data beyond what the source licence permits.

**5. Clinician raters (evidence-sufficiency study only).** Blinded clinician review of model outputs constituted expert professional assessment of AI-generated text, not research *on* the clinicians. No personal or sensitive data about the raters were collected beyond ordinary contributor information, and no patient data were shown to them.

## Consequence

Because the research does not involve personal data, interventions on humans, or human biological material, it does not constitute research requiring ethical approval under lag 2003:460. This is consistent with the manuscripts' statements that the studies used public, deidentified questions and generated outputs, with no patient contact, intervention, or identifiable health records.

Should a journal, preprint server, or other party require a formal determination, the author will seek an advisory statement (rådgivande yttrande) from Etikprövningsmyndigheten.

## Retention

This assessment is retained in the study records of both replication packages and will be provided to editors or reviewers on request.
